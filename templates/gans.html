GANs

GANs (Generative Adversarial Networks) are a class of machine learning models that consist of two neural networks — the Generator and the Discriminator — that are trained simultaneously in a competitive framework. They are widely used for tasks such as image generation, style transfer, and data augmentation.

GANs

The GAN architecture consists of:

  • Generator (G):
    1. Generator learns to generate synthetic data that resembles the real data distribution.
    2. It takes in random noise (latent space) as input and tries to generate data (like images) that are indistinguishable from real data.
    3. Its objective is to fool the Discriminator into thinking its output is real.

  • Discriminator (D):
    1. The Discriminator is a binary classifier that distinguishes between real and fake data.
    2. It takes in real data (from the training set) and synthetic data (generated by the Generator) and predicts whether each piece of data is real or fake.
    3. Its objective is to correctly classify real data as real and fake data as fake.


  • Training:
    • During training, the Generator generates fake data from random noise.
    • The Discriminator evaluates the fake data alongside real data and learns to distinguish between them.
    • The two networks are in a zero-sum game: The Generator is trying to maximize the probability of the Discriminator making an error (i.e., classifying fake data as real). The Discriminator is trying to minimize this error.

In an ideal scenario, both the Generator and Discriminator reach a state where the Generator produces data indistinguishable from real data, and the Discriminator cannot differentiate between them.
